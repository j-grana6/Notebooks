Stochastic Finite Transducer Nets and Event Driven Game Theory¶

SFT-Nets¶

  • Directed (generally not acyclic) Graph
  • Nodes
    • Internal States

  • Messages

  • Events

    • Change of state or sending of a message
    • Spontaneous or Stimulated
  • Rates
    • Rate at which an event happens
  • Event Distribution
    • Given an event happens, determines which event happens.

With an SFT-Net specified (with the event rates Poisson), it is possible to specify a generator matrix that governs the state of the system.

Brief aside ...¶

Recall that in the discrete time representation, the system evolves according to a probability transition matrix given by:

$\mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} a_{1,1} &\ldots &a_{1,n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a_{n,1} & \ldots & a_{n,n}\\ \end{bmatrix}$

The continuous time analog is called a \textbf{generator} or transition rate matrix given by

$\mathbf{Q} = \begin{bmatrix} -\sum_{i\neq 1}^n \lambda_{1,i} &\ldots &\lambda_{1,n} \\ \vdots & -\sum_{i\neq 2}^n \lambda_{2,i}& \vdots \\ \lambda_{n,1} & \ldots & -\sum_{i\neq 3}^n \lambda_{2,i} \\ \end{bmatrix}$

The generator completely governs the evolution of the system:

$P(X(t) = x | X(0)) = [e^{t\mathbf{Q}}]_{X(0)}$

Game Theory?¶

  • SFTs have elements controlled by humans as to maximize an expected utility.

Outline of Initial Optimization Experiments¶

  • SFTs are either a hosts, an attacker or a defender.
  • The defender uses a simple likelihood detector and it is fixed.
  • The attacker receives a reward upon infecting some hosts.
  • The rate at which he "get's the opportunity" to send a message is pre-set.
  • When he get's the opportunity to send a message, he decides to send a message along an edge or to do nothing at all.
  • The strategies are computed using Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS)
