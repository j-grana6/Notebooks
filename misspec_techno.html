"Caterpillar Topology"¶

In this version of model misspecification, the attacker actually explores the network according to the caterpillar pattern. That is, when he infects a new node, he begins sending messages out to all connected nodes. However, in our "attacker model" we only assume the attacker traverses the center path. The rationale for this is that sometimes, we might have a network that is too large to develop an attacker model. Therefore, we might simply assign a rate of 0 to edges into a host that we are less concerned about but continue to monitor them and include them in our likelihood calculations. Below is a figure of the topology and the results.

The rate of clean message transmission is 1. The rate of malicious message transmission along the center path is .5 and the rate of malicious message transmission to the left and right nodes is .25.

Mean zero noise¶

In this experiment, I simply add mean zero noise to each of the rates of the true attacker model described above. The obvious reason for this model of misspecification is that we might, on average, know how the attacker will traverse the network but we will not know exactly. The standard distribution is 10% of the actual rate.
