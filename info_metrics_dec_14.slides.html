Summer 2014

Outline¶

  • Net-Form Games (and Multi-Agent influence diagrams)

  • The Level-K Quantal Response Model

Didactic Example¶

  • Two firms must simultaneously choose a production quantity from a finite set to maximize profits.
  • Demand can be either 'High' or 'Low', each representing a different demand function.
  • Firm 1 observes the market demand function perfectly
  • Firm 2 does not know the demand function exactly but receives a signal of 'H' or 'L'.

Level-K¶

  • Bounded Rationality
  • Beauty Contest Game

Assumption Set 2¶

  • The level-0 distribution is known (relaxable(!))
  • The expected utilities of any strategy are computable (implies the solution to the game is known)

Data characteristics and a note on learning¶

  • Data is observations of the game
  • No learning

Question 1¶

How can we estimate the level's of the players?

$\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{k}}\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{p, u}} D(\mathbf{p}||q_k) + D(\mathbf{u}||\mathbf{u_0})$

subject to

The probabilities equal the observed probabilities plus noise (the data)

\begin{align} \min_\mathbf{K}\min_{\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{u}} \mathbf{D}(\mathbf{p} || \mathbf{q_k}) + \mathbf{D}(\mathbf{u} || \mathbf{u_0})&= \Bigg[\sum_{I}\sum_{X_{pa(i)}}\sum_{X_i} p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}\log(\frac{p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}}{q_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},k}}) \; +\\ & \sum_{I}\sum_{X_{pa(i)}}\sum_{X_i}\sum_w u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w}\log(u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w})\Bigg] \nonumber \\ \text{ s.t.} \nonumber \\ d_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}&= p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}} + \sum_wv_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w}u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \forall i,x_i,x_{pa(i)} \nonumber \\ 1&= \sum_{X_i}p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \forall x_i,x_{pa(i)} \nonumber \\ 1&= \sum_wu_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w} \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\; \forall i,x_i,x_{pa(i)} \label{gme1} \end{align}

Issues¶

  • In the level-k model, the conditional distributions are degenerate!
  • In real data, the distirbutions are not.
  • Why?

Quantal Response¶

  • The gist: Actions with higher utilities get a higher probability of occurring.
  • Formally: Given a finite set of actions $\{x_1, x_2... x_n\}$ that yield expected utilities $\{y_1, y_2 ...y_n\}$ the probability the agent takes action $x_i$ is given by:

\begin{equation} P(x_i) = \frac{e^{\beta y_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^n e^{\beta y_j}} \end{equation}

GME with Quantal Response Prior¶

$\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{k}}\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{p, u, \beta}} D(\mathbf{p}||q_k(\beta)) + D(\mathbf{u}||\mathbf{u_0})$

subject to

  • The probabilities equal the observed probabilities plus noise (the data)
  • The prior probabilitiea are defined by the quantal response probabilities with parameter $\beta$.

\begin{equation} \displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{k}}\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{p, u, \beta}} D(\mathbf{p}||q_k(\beta)) + D(\mathbf{u}||\mathbf{u_0}) = \Bigg[\sum_{I}\sum_{X_{pa(i)}}\sum_{X_i} p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}\log(\frac{p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}}{q_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},k}(\beta_i)}) + \sum_{I}\sum_{X_{pa(i)}}\sum_{X_i}\sum_w u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w}\log(u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w})\Bigg] \end{equation}

Subject to

\begin{align} d_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}= p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}} + \sum_wv_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w}u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w} \; \; &\forall i,x_i,x_{pa(i)} \nonumber \\ 1= \sum_{X_i}p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}} \; \; &\forall i, X_i,x_{pa(i)} \nonumber \\ 1= \sum_wu_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w} \; \; &\forall i,x_i,x_{pa(i)} \end{align}

Relaxing Assumptions¶

Utility function Relaxation¶

  • The researcher knows the utility functions up to a set of parameters, $\theta$
  • $EU[x_i] = f(\theta, ...)$

$\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{k}}\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{p, u, \beta, \theta}} D(\mathbf{p}||q_k(\beta, \theta)) + D(\mathbf{u}||\mathbf{u_0})$

subject to

  • The probabilities equal the observed probabilities plus noise (the data)
  • The prior probabilitiea are defined by the quantal response probabilities with parameter $\beta$.

Relaxing the Level-0 Assumption¶

  • Assumption: The level-k expected utilities can be written as a function of the level-0 distribution.
  • $\mathbf{q}_k = f_k(\mathbf{p_0})$

The new formulation becomes:

$\displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{k}}\displaystyle\max_{\mathbf{p, u}} H(\mathbf{p}) + H(\mathbf{u})$

subject to

$f_k(\mathbf{p}) =$ observed probabilities + noise

\begin{equation} \displaystyle\min_{\mathbf{k}}\displaystyle\max_{\mathbf{p, u}} H(\mathbf{p}) + H(\mathbf{u}) = \Bigg[\sum_{I}\sum_{X_{pa(i)}}\sum_{X_i} p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}\log(p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}) + \sum_{I}\sum_{X_{pa(i)}}\sum_{X_i}\sum_w u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w}\log(u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w})\Bigg] \end{equation}

subject to

\begin{align} d_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}}= f_k(\mathbf{p}_{i,x_{pa(i)}}) + \sum_wv_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w}u_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w} \; \; &\forall i,x_i,x_{pa(i)} \nonumber \\ 1= \sum_{X_i}p_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)}} \; \; &\forall i, X_i,x_{pa(i)} \nonumber \\ 1= \sum_wu_{i,x_i,x_{pa(i)},w} \; \; &\forall i,x_i,x_{pa(i)} \end{align}

Level-K: Rock-paper-scissors¶

  • Augmented so there is a reward for playing and in ties, both players win
  • Instead of logit Quantal Response, linear quantal response: $P(x_i)= \frac{EU[x_i]}{\sum_jEU[x_j]}$

L1 Rock prob = (p_s+p_r-pp+1)/4 L2 Roch prib = (3ps-Pr-pp)/7
